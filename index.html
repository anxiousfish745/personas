P1. Preparation

Accessible interface

I choose CVS self-checkout as the observed accessible interface.

Questions

  • Are you a student? Please tell me a little about yourself.
  • Have you previously used the self-checkout before? If so, how often do you use the self-checkout?
  • Is there something that's getting you stuck in the checkout process, or is there anything you need that's hard to spot?
  • What do you like/ dislike about the self-checkout?

Sketch and key interactive components

In general, there are these four key interactive components in the interface:

  • The scanning prompts
    1. Scan the card for applicable promos and coupons
    2. Scan the items for check-out
    3. Scan the paper coupons for discounts
  • The confirmations
    1. Confirm the scanned items on the left side
    2. Confirm whether the user wants to apply the coupon
    3. Confirm the following step
  • The payment selection

    • Prompt the user for the final step of payment

  • The get help button

    • Exists in every step so that the user can get help from the staff no matter which part confuses them

P3. Personas

-Jesse-

Briefly describe the user (1 sentence headline)

Jesse is a sophomore college student who just wants to drop by CVS after having dinner outside and on her way back to the dorm.

Point out the interface problems that your personas face.

Jesse is having trouble with the overwhelming options since she does not know how to use the coupons that is shown on the price tags. Also when she tries to put the scanned items into her backpack, the self-checkout machine keeps alarming that she should place them into the bagging area, which makes her feel really awkward.

Explain why and how a given persona represents the users of your chosen interface.

There is a large amount of young customers who just want to browse and do some random shopping in a drug store, and they do not want to spend too much time in the check out. They might not be frequent visitors, and they want to see if there are any deals. Jesse fits into this type of users who is more receptive to using self-checkout, but not familiar with the process.

-Ben-

Briefly describe the user (1 sentence headline)

Ben is a young professional in his 30s. He is on his way home from gym after work, and wants to quickly grab some snacks on his way home for a live football game.

Point out the interface problems that your personas face.

Ben scans the items (two energy bars) and places them aside, and the voice loudly prompts him to place them in the bagging area, while he does not need the bags. He is also in a hurry while the interface keeps prompting selections and buttons that he does not need.

Explain why and how a given persona represents the users of your chosen interface.

There are times that users want to achieve their goals as soon as possible—for example, temporarily parking where they shouldn't have, or on their way to another thing. People in a hurry with only a few things in their cart might consider self-checkout as a good option since it eliminates the waiting line(sometimes) and small talk. Ben is this type of user—tired and in a hurry—and will get frustrated when the interface and checkout process get too complicated.
